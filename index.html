LLM INFRA

Feedback‑driven LLM optimization

tensorzero/tensorzero

TensorZero creates a feedback loop for optimizing LLM applications, turning production data into smarter, faster, and cheaper models. It bridges the gap between model‑as‑service and real‑world deployment telemetry.

6,834☆
Language: Rust

How to use this: treat TensorZero as a lightweight “LLM ops” layer.

For your trading or data‑processing pipelines, think of it as the place where you log every model call, its outcome, and downstream impact (e.g., PnL attribution, latency, user feedback). Then you can replay and tune prompts, sampling parameters, and routing decisions in a way that’s closer to A/B testing than blind prompting.

Agent‑Zero AI framework

A lightweight AI‑agent framework that lets you compose autonomous loops around language models, tools, and memory. Designed for rapid iteration and minimal boilerplate.

9,785☆
Language: Python

How to use this: prototype trading or research agents without building plumbing first.

You can plug in your own executors for “see markets → trade → record results → refine logic” as a cyclic agent. Use it to test ideas like overnight scanner‑to‑order workflows, or daily research summarization agents, then migrate the core logic into your main stack once the pattern is stable.

NETWORKING

Secure overlay network with SSO

netbirdio/netbird

NetBird connects your devices into a secure WireGuard‑based overlay network with SSO, MFA, and granular access controls, replacing VPNs and manual firewall rules.

15,603☆
Language: Go

How to use this: treat NetBird as your “zero‑trust dev network.”

For your dev and trading‑tool stack, this lets you keep sensitive data and tools on isolated machines while accessing them from any device as if they were on the same LAN. Consider using it to connect your backtesting servers, private dashboards, and development rigs without exposing ports to the public internet.
